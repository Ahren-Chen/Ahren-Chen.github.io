Summary

I decided to take a co-op course in the first semester of grade 11, and luckily, I managed to get a position as a student IT assistant at Dr. Bette Stephenson's Centre for Learning. My duties were mainly focused on troubleshooting laptops/desktops and resetting them to make them usable; the repaired laptops/desktops would be shipped out to teachers who need a replacement. I also got to experience working with servers and networks briefly with others. The vast majority of issues had to do with hardware issues, so replacing/fixing the hardware was the bulk of my experience, but I also got to experience imaging and manipulating BIOS settings.

What I have Learned

The schedule consisted of my friend Adam in the morning followed by me in the afternoon, and although we both had our responsibilities, when we were unable to complete our tasks, we would leave a message on what needed to be done for the other person to see. This inspired teamwork, collaboration and increased our overall efficiency since there was never any confusion on what still needed to be completed. We often worked on similar long-term projects, and whenever we met in the short period before we swapped, we would chat and catch up as friends. I looked forward to every day there and continued to learn, often, I was left to my own devices once the task was assigned to me, so I had the chance to explore different technologies on my own.

Me and my teammate with all the laptops we fixed

The independence to do what I wanted as long as the final result was satisfactory gave me a lot of freedom to research and try different things to see what worked and what didn't. There were extremely competent veterans in the field around me that were always ready to help and give advice whenever I needed it, and I truly learned by doing. I even got to disassemble hard drives and other hardware to put my understanding of their concepts into practical use. I am forever grateful that I got to meet such amazing people and learn more than I could have imagined from them.
